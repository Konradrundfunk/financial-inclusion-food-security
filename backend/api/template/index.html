The importance of supporting smallholder farmers

Most effective against poverty.

Growth in agriculture is up to 3.2 times better at reducing headcount poverty in low-income countries compared to any other sector in the economy, including commerce, manufacturing, and construction (Christiaensen et al. 2011). This is because agriculture is a leading source of jobs for the poor. For example, 84% of the extreme poor in Africa live in rural areas and are employed in farming activities (Beegle, K. and Christiaensen, L. 2019)

Key to the global food supply.

There are 500 million smallholder farmers globally, most of them located in the developing world, accounting for ca. 40% of the global food supply.



Problem: Lack of access to financial services.

There are a range of reasons why it is hard to be a smallholder farmer in rural, sparsely populated areas in developing economies. The consequences of climate change like droughts, extreme weather events and desertification made it only more difficult in recent years.
Most of the smallholder farmers do not have access to adequate financial services that could help them expand their enterprises and support their families. Especially in the case of credit local financial institutions face the challenge of high operational costs to reach the farmers but also the fact that many farmers pose too high a risk of commercially oriented credit providers.
